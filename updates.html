2021 Innovire Summit Thank You!!

On Saturday, June 5th and Sunday, June 6th, 2021, Innovire hosted the 2021 Innovire Summit. It was our third annual and first ever virtual Innovire Summit! It was an amazing experience with over 60 attendees, 20 volunteers and 16 guest speakers and workshop presenters.

Thank you to everyone that attended the 2021 Innovire Summit. We truly hope everyone had a great time and felt inspired to innovate and create the future!

We would like to give a shoutout to our amazing volunteers who took time out to support our event and helped keep everything running as smoothly as possible!

Special thanks to all the incredible guest speakers and workshop presenters for taking the time out of your busy schedules to inspire and excite everyone about STEM and its future!

Thank you to our partners for the event Volunteering Peel and Youth Culture for supporting us and believing in us, we couldn’t have done it without you!

Lastly, a big thank you goes out to our amazing team here at Innovire who have worked tirelessly to bring this event to fruition and it wouldn’t have been possible without them.

Special shoutouts go to Rishi Jarajapu for being an amazing MC and Kim Cooper for mentoring and inspiring us to make it all possible!

Innovire Celebrates Its 3 Year Anniversary!!

In May Innovire celebrated its third anniversary and it’s been an amazing journey so far. Innovire has grown so much since it began in 2018, and our team has been able to impact and connect with 600+ youth. The last year and a half has been a roller coaster ride filled with doubts and uncertainties but through it all we persevered and put forward initiatives to inspire youth to innovate and give them access to the resources, skills, experiences and knowledge they’d need to do so. While moving to a virtual setting has made it difficult to build and interact with our community, genuine passion for inspiring youth, creativity, innovation, and perseverance allowed our team to continue providing valuable opportunities for youth in our community.

The entire journey has truly been a dream come true, and none of it would have been possible without everyone who has supported us and helped us get this far. I’d like to thank everyone who has contributed to our journey. Thank you to our mentors - Mrs. Darroch, Mrs. Jackson, Mr. Jansma, Mrs. Perez and the rest of the SciTech staff at Tomken Road Middle School, Kamran Shaikh, Nadeem Nathoo, Professor Soo Min Toh, Carol Solonenko - who have supported and guided us from the very beginning. They always push us, advise us and open up opportunities for us. Thank you to Youth Culture, Volunteering Peel, IMI at UTM and all of our partners and sponsors for believing in our mission and making our goals a reality.

Thank you to every guest speaker and presenter who has partaken in our initiatives for always bringing our audience and organization the best content, knowledge, stories, projects, initiatives and advice (Kim Cooper, Nadeem Nathoo, Alan Smithson, Dr. Cynthia Goh, Jaime Casap, Kyla Bolden, Erik Lloyd, Megan Gran, Adalyn Ordono, Arthur Chen, Professor Ulrich Krull, Professor Gideon Humphrey, Dr. David Jansma, Karen Ho James, Zaynah Bhanji, Areez Bhanji, Ayaan Esmail, Zain Lakhani, Zara Syed, Arnav Shah, Gira Chawda, Varsha Prasad, Anuraj Shah, Jaimil Dalwadi, Sri Anumakonda). Thank you to Khushi Patel, Elle Kerr, Ankur Boyed, Andrew Wang, Rishi Jarajapu, Ali Lakhani, Matthew Tang, Noah Ma, Lily James, Rohith Rajmohan and the rest of our amazing team that makes all of Innovire's initiatives happen through countless hours of hard work and dedication.

Most of all I’d like to thank each and every person who has participated in our initiatives and make them all so successful. The Innovire Community has grown so much and is made up of youth with a wide range of talent, passions and aspirations. We truly hope we can continue to inspire youth to innovate solutions to problems faced by our world and build a community of innovators and change-makers.

As Innovire gears up for its 4th year, we continue to reflect on what we’ve done so far and how much more we still have to go. We look forward to continuing our work to achieve our vision! We will be opening up applications for new members soon, so stay tuned!!

- Abdullah Sabole & Khushi Patel

COVID-19 Update

As we are in the midst of a pandemic, this past year and in the year going forward we have been trying to adapt to the situation. In doing so, we had to put a pause on our yearly in person Innovire Summit but we have come up with various plans and initiatives that will help us to build our brand awareness and see through our dream of inspiring youth to innovate using technology.

Our Take On Inequality

We stand against racism and inequality, and as an organization meant to inspire students to innovate, we do NOT condone any form of racial bias or discrimination. We encourage you all to educate yourselves and acknowledge the privilege you might have. It is essential to create a more inclusive and accepting community for everyone.

The injustices happening globally have brought many disturbing issues to light, they are disgraceful and it is our (and your) responsibility to change that. We support you and all those who have been impacted by these injustices.

We at the Innovire Team would like to inform our audiences of ways that you can inform yourself and help support these causes. For a list of petitions and various sources regarding these movements, we have created an organized list, and we highly suggest you check all of these links out: https://innovire-resources.carrd.co/
